The Path

How I got here

2016
Graduated — Royal College of Music, London

Composition degree with a focus on contemporary film and media scoring. Thesis project: a 40-minute score for a student feature screened at BFI Southbank.

2017–2019
Orchestrator — Paramount Europe

Three years orchestrating for senior composers on theatrical releases. Learned how scoring decisions are made under pressure, with picture locked and release dates firm.

2020
First solo credit — The Weight of Stars (documentary)

Won Best Sound at the Edinburgh Documentary Film Festival. The project that made me commit fully to independent composing.

2022–Present
Independent Composer — London & Berlin

Working across film, games, and trailers with directors and studios I admire. Limited to 4–6 major projects per year.

Craft

The instruments, digital and acoustic

I work in the space between the orchestra and the synthesizer. Here's what's in the room.

DAW & Production

Logic Pro X, Nuendo 13. Templates built over 8 years for fast iteration without sacrificing depth.

Sample Libraries

Spitfire Symphonic Orchestra, BBCSO, Orchestral Tools Berlin — plus several private libraries I've built from live sessions.

Synthesis

Eurorack modular system (300+ modules), Korg MS-20, Roland Juno-106. Hardware first, then digital processing.

Game Integration

Wwise Certified Developer. Delivered adaptive audio systems for 3 commercial game releases.

Live Recording

Studio piano (Bösendorfer 225), cello, duduk, and access to session musicians across London and Berlin.

Delivery Formats

Stems, stems+ambiences, full mixes, M&E tracks, and Wwise-ready audio files. All metadata tagged and organized.
