How It Works

From brief to final mix

Every score follows the same three-stage process — built around clarity, collaboration, and zero surprises at delivery.

01

The Brief

We start with a conversation — not a form. I want to understand what you need audiences to feel, not just what genre you're working in. Notes, references, and instincts all welcome.

02

The Composition

I work in focused bursts, sharing sketch demos early so you're never waiting long to hear a direction. Two rounds of revision are included — most projects need one.

03

The Delivery

Stems, full mixes, M&E tracks, and Wwise-ready files — all metadata tagged, organized, and delivered ahead of schedule. No chasing, no ambiguity.

Craft

The instruments, digital and acoustic

I work in the space between the orchestra and the synthesizer. Here's what's in the room.

DAW & Production

Logic Pro X, Nuendo 13. Templates built over 8 years for fast iteration without sacrificing depth.

Sample Libraries

Spitfire Symphonic Orchestra, BBCSO, Orchestral Tools Berlin — plus several private libraries I've built from live sessions.

Synthesis

Eurorack modular system (300+ modules), Korg MS-20, Roland Juno-106. Hardware first, then digital processing.

Game Integration

Wwise Certified Developer. Delivered adaptive audio systems for 3 commercial game releases.

Live Recording

Studio piano (Bösendorfer 225), cello, duduk, and access to session musicians across London and Berlin.

Delivery Formats

Stems, stems+ambiences, full mixes, M&E tracks, and Wwise-ready audio files. All metadata tagged and organized.
