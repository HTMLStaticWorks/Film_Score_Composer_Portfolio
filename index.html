The Composer

A cinematic ear, a technical mind

"I grew up watching films twice — once for the story, once for what the music was doing to me."

I'm Marcus Hale, a composer based between London and Berlin. I studied composition at the Royal College of Music before spending years as an orchestrator for larger productions — learning how music earns its place in a story frame by frame.

Today I work independently on projects I believe in: films that take emotional risks, games that build worlds, and trailers that demand attention. My process starts with a conversation about what you want audiences to feel — not just hear.

Behind the score

01

Dusk Protocol

Feature Film Hybrid 2024

Director Priya Anand wanted a score that felt like the city itself had a heartbeat — synthetic but wounded. I built the main theme around a descending minor third played on a detuned cimbalom, layered with modular pulse sequences.

The result was a score that audiences described as "the most unsettling lullaby they'd ever heard" — which was exactly the brief.

02

Echoes of Veil

Video Game Fantasy 2023

For this open-world RPG, I composed 4.2 hours of adaptive music — 68 stems that the Wwise middleware blends in real-time based on player proximity, combat state, and weather conditions.

The main overworld theme was written for solo duduk and string quartet, then expanded into full orchestral versions for key story moments. Players reported being emotionally caught off guard by transitions they couldn't anticipate.

03

The Weight of Stars

Documentary Ambient 2022

A feature documentary about the last generation of analog astronomers, this score needed to feel both intimate and cosmic. I recorded an upright piano with half its strings damped with felt, then processed it through a reverb unit designed for cathedral acoustics.

The score won Best Sound at the Edinburgh Documentary Film Festival — the director described it as "the sound of human curiosity."
